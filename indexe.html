BaZi Analysis in 7 Steps

Learn BaZi destiny analysis from scratch with this step-by-step guide

7 Steps to Master BaZi Analysis

1

Step 1: Set Up the BaZi Chart

A BaZi chart consists of Year, Month, Day, and Hour pillars, each with a Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch, totaling eight characters - hence "BaZi" (Eight Characters).

Pillar Year Month Day Hour
Stem 壬 戊 甲 丙
Branch 戌 申 寅 寅
Hidden Stems 戊辛丁 庚壬戊 甲丙戊 甲丙戊

Example:

For someone born on August 15, 1995 at 10:00 AM:

  1. Year Pillar: 1995 is Yi Hai year (based on solar terms)
  2. Month Pillar: August is Shen month (after LiQiu)
  3. Day Pillar: Calculated using formula or calendar
  4. Hour Pillar: 10 AM is Si hour

Final BaZi: Yi Hai, Jia Shen, Wu Yin, Ding Si

2

Step 2: Determine Daymaster Strength

The Daymaster is the Heavenly Stem of the Day Pillar, representing the self. Analyzing its strength is fundamental to BaZi interpretation.

Four Strength Criteria

  1. Seasonal Strength: Daymaster's element matches or is supported by the month branch
  2. Root Support: Branches contain the Daymaster's root (e.g., Wood in Yin-Mao)
  3. Peer Support: Stems with same element (Comparisons/Rob Wealth)
  4. Nourishment: Presence of productive elements (Resource stars)

Strength Assessment Mnemonic

"Three strong, one weak, two means balanced"

  • 3-4 criteria met: Strong
  • 2 criteria met: Balanced
  • 0-1 criteria met: Weak

Case Analysis:

BaZi: Ren Xu, Wu Shen, Jia Yin, Bing Yin

  • Jia Wood in Shen month (Metal controls Wood): Seasonally weak
  • Day branch Yin Wood, Hour branch Yin Wood: Root support
  • No Wood in stems: No peer support
  • Ren Water produces Jia Wood: Nourishment
  • Conclusion: Balanced leaning weak

Step 4: Analyze Element Balance

Assess the waxing and waning of the Five Elements (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) in the chart.

Element Strength

  • Wood: Yin-Mao-Chen strong, Hai-Zi supporting
  • Fire: Si-Wu-Wei strong, Yin-Mao supporting
  • Earth: Chen-Xu-Chou-Wei strong, Si-Wu supporting
  • Metal: Shen-You-Xu strong, Chen-Chou supporting
  • Water: Hai-Zi-Chou strong, Shen-You supporting

Element Relationships

Productive Cycle: Wood→Fire→Earth→Metal→Water→Wood

Controlling Cycle: Wood→Earth→Water→Fire→Metal→Wood

Element balance is key to chart assessment

Step 6: Determine Useful and Harmful Elements

Useful elements benefit the chart, while harmful elements create imbalance.

Useful Element Selection

  • Strong Daymaster: Controlling (Authority), Output, Wealth elements
  • Weak Daymaster: Nourishing (Resource), Supporting (Comparison) elements
  • Special Patterns: Follow the pattern's needs

Harmful Elements

  • Elements that control useful elements
  • Elements that worsen imbalance
  • Problematic Ten Gods for the Daymaster

Useful Element Example:

BaZi: Ren Xu, Wu Shen, Jia Yin, Bing Yin

  • Daymaster: Jia Wood (balanced leaning weak)
  • Issue: Strong Wood, weak Metal-Water
  • Useful: Metal (controls Wood), Water (produces Wood)
  • Harmful: Wood (Comparisons), Fire (Output)
7

Step 7: Analyze Luck Cycles

Luck Pillars (10-year cycles) and Annual Pillars are crucial for timing predictions.

Luck Pillars

  • Function: 10-year cycles affecting long-term destiny
  • Calculation: Yang year males forward, Yin year females forward; Yin year males backward, Yang year females backward
  • Effects:
    • Useful element in power: Good decade
    • Harmful element in power: Challenging decade
    • Luck clashes chart: Major changes

Annual Pillars

  • Function: Yearly fluctuations, timing of events
  • Assessment:
    • Useful element year: Smooth year
    • Harmful element year: Difficult year
    • Year clashes: Sudden changes
    • Year combinations: Relationship shifts
